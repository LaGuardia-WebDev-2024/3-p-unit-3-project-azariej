Recipe #1: Salmon Maki

  • Time: 1 hour total (includes prep)
  • Serves: 1
Ingredients | Quantity
Salmon | Sashimi grade / about one chunck per roll
Nori | One sheet
Sushi Rice | About a cup per roll

Prep: Cook the sushi rice
Step 1: Lay a sheet of nori on a bamboo rolling mat, shiny side down. Put the cooked sushi rice onto the nori and spread to cover ¾ of the nori.
Step 2: Lay the strips of smoked salmon halfway along the rice.

Recipe #2: Spaghetti

  • Time: 45 Mins
  • Serves: 5
Ingredients | Quantity
Speghetti Noodles(450g) | One box
Ground Meat (450g) | One Package
Tomatos & Tomato Sauce | One can/ A few baby tomatos/ about half of a normal sized tomato Spices | As much as you'd like

Step 1: Heat the oil in a large pot over medium-high heat
Step 2: Add the meat and cook until browned, about 8 minutes. Use a wooden spoon to break the meat into smaller crumbles as the meat cooks.
Step 3: Add the onions and cook, stirring every once in a while, until softened, about 5 minutes.
Step 4: Using a bamboo rolling mqerbat, roll up from the bottom, tightly away from you (a). Leave a small amount of nori unrolled (b). Wet this part of the nori with water and finish rolling to seal it. Then cut
Step 5: Stir in the garlic, tomato paste, oregano, and red pepper flakes and cook, stirring continuously for about 1 minute.
Step 6: Pour in the water and use a wooden spoon to scrape up any bits of meat or onion stuck to the bottom of the pot.
Step 7: Stir in the tomatoes, 3/4 teaspoon of salt, and a generous pinch of black pepper.
Step 2: Bring the sauce to a low simmer. Cook uncovered for 25 minutes. As it cooks, stir and taste the sauce a few times so you can adjust the seasoning accordingly (see notes for seasoning suggestions).
Step 2: About 15 minutes before the spaghetti sauce finishes cooking, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Then, cook the pasta according to the package directions, but check for doneness a minute or two before the suggested cooking time.
Step 2: Remove the sauce from the heat and stir in the basil. Toss in the cooked pasta and leave for a minute so that it absorbs some of the sauce. Toss again, and then serve with grated parmesan cheese on top.
